## Introduction
The Magic Mouse is a wireless mouse developed by Apple. It features multi-touch capabilities and is designed for use with macOS and iOS devices. This document provides a comprehensive guide to setting up and using the Magic Mouse effectively.

## System Requirements
- macOS 10.10 or later
- iPadOS 13.4 or later (for iPad users)
- Bluetooth enabled device

## Setup Instructions
### Connecting via Bluetooth
1. **Turn on your Magic Mouse**: Locate the switch on the bottom of the mouse and toggle it to turn it on.
2. **Open Bluetooth Preferences on your device**:
   - On macOS: Go to `System Preferences` > `Bluetooth`.
   - On iPadOS: Open `Settings` > `Bluetooth`.
3. **Pair the Magic Mouse**:
   - When the device is turned on, it should appear in the list of available devices. Click `Connect` next to the Magic Mouse.

### Charging the Magic Mouse
1. **Locate the Lightning connector**: The Lightning port is located on the bottom of the mouse.
2. **Charge your mouse**: Use a Lightning cable to connect the mouse to a power source. A full charge can last up to 30 days, depending on usage.

## Usage Instructions
### Basic Functions
- **Click**: Press down on the top of the mouse to perform a left-click.
- **Right-click**: Click on the right side of the mouse to perform a right-click. Ensure that your settings allow for right-click functionality.

### Gestures
- **Scroll**: Use one finger to glide up and down the mouse surface to scroll through pages.
- **Swipe**: Swipe left or right using one finger to navigate between pages or apps.
- **Zoom**: Double-tap with two fingers to zoom in or out on content.

## Troubleshooting
- **Mouse Not Connecting**: Ensure that Bluetooth is enabled on your device and that the Magic Mouse is powered on. Try turning the mouse off and on again.
- **Intermittent Connectivity**: Ensure that the mouse is charged, and check for interference from other wireless devices.

## FAQs
**Q1: How do I reset my Magic Mouse?**  
A1: To reset, turn the mouse off, wait for a few seconds, and then turn it back on.

**Q2: Can I use the Magic Mouse with non-Apple devices?**  
A2: Yes, the Magic Mouse can connect to any Bluetooth-enabled device, although some features may be limited.

**Q3: What should I do if my Magic Mouse isn’t charging?**  
A3: Check if the cable is functional and ensure that the Lightning port is clean from debris.
